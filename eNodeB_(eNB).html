eNodeB (eNB)

Category: 4G / architecture
Executive Summary:
The eNodeB is the 4G LTE base station hardware within the radio access network. It manages radio resource allocation and physical user connectivity.

Advanced Technical Definition

Operating dynamically within the E-UTRAN, the eNB leverages advanced physical layer technologies like OFDM, OFDMA, MIMO, and Carrier Aggregation to reach LTE-A speeds of 3 Gbps. It communicates heavily with the MME to orchestrate hard and soft handovers. In complex HetNet deployments, macro eNBs coordinate with small cells using SON, ICIC, and FFR to actively suppress edge-cell interference and purify the RF link.
