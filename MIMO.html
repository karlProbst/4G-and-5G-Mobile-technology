MIMO

Category: 4G / radio
Executive Summary:
Multiple Input Multiple Output (MIMO) utilizes multiple uncorrelated antennas at the transmitter and receiver. It multiplies data capacity via spatial diversity without requiring additional frequency bandwidth.

Advanced Technical Definition

By leveraging spatial multiplexing and mathematical precoding, MIMO transmits distinct parallel data streams across the exact same frequency channel. LTE-Advanced specifies up to 8x8 MIMO for downlink, allowing 4G and LTE-Advanced systems to break the gigabit barrier. It remains fundamental to 5G-NR operations, acting as the foundation for gNB beamforming in both sub-6 GHz and mmWave frequencies.
