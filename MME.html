MME

Category: 4G / architecture
Executive Summary:
The Mobility Management Entity (MME) is the primary control node for the 4G LTE network. It processes signaling, authenticates users, and manages roaming mobility.

Advanced Technical Definition

Situated strategically in the Evolved Packet Core (EPC), the MME strictly handles control-plane NAS (Non-Access Stratum) signaling between the UE and the network. It continuously consults the HSS via the S6a interface to extract user cryptographic keys and authorize access. Unlike the 5G architecture where functions are decoupled into the AMF, the MME centrally anchors EPS bearers and coordinates inter-eNodeB (eNB) handovers.
