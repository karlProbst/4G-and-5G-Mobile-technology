OFDM

Category: 4G / radio
Executive Summary:
Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiplexing (OFDM) is a digital multi-carrier modulation scheme. It transmits data over closely spaced, orthogonal subcarriers to resist interference.

Advanced Technical Definition

Utilized continuously by the eNodeB (eNB), OFDM mitigates narrowband interference and frequency-selective fading. It overlaps subcarriers mathematically such that the side lobes of one carrier align perfectly with the zero-crossings of adjacent peaks, allowing for high-density spectral packing. It is the foundational RF building block behind 4G, LTE-Advanced, OFDMA, and the 5G-NR.html">5G-NR scalable numerology.
