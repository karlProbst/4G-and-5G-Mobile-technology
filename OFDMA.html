OFDMA

Category: 4G / radio
Executive Summary:
Orthogonal Frequency Division Multiple Access (OFDMA) is the multi-user evolution of OFDM. It assigns subsets of subcarriers to different users dynamically across time and frequency.

Advanced Technical Definition

OFDMA partitions the overall frequency band into Resource Blocks (RBs) spanning both frequency subcarriers and timeslots. The eNodeB (eNB) dynamically allocates these blocks based on real-time channel state information, maximizing E-UTRAN spectral efficiency. This multi-user scheduling prevents overlapping interference and is the primary multiplexing technique for modern mobile data streams.
