Carrier Aggregation

Category: 4G / radio
Executive Summary:
Carrier Aggregation bonds multiple discrete frequency bands into a single, wider data pipeline. It is the primary technology that allows LTE-Advanced to achieve massive multi-gigabit throughput.

Advanced Technical Definition

Introduced in 3GPP Release 10, Carrier Aggregation supports intra-band contiguous, intra-band non-contiguous, and inter-band spectrum combinations. By bonding up to five 20 MHz component carriers (totaling 100 MHz bandwidth), it drastically expands capacity within the E-UTRAN. The eNodeB (eNB) uses this in tandem with MIMO to satisfy the aggressive peak data requirements of the Evolved Packet System (EPS).
